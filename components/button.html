Buttons. Five tiers. One scarcity rule.

First base component. Consumes the locked semantic tokens from Color v1. Five tiers enforce the scarcity rule: Signal is rare, Terracotta is the default primary, Success/Warning/Info are not button colors. Three sizes (benchmarked to Claude, Notion, Linear), six states, five variants per tier. 6px radius, Inter 500. Full spec: button.md.

Signal is reserved. Used on the logo tile and on the single hero CTA per marketing page. Never on product surfaces. Terracotta is the default positive action in products. Destructive carries irreversible weight only. Ghost sits next to primary as the dismiss path. Neutral handles low-emphasis persist (save draft, export, back).

Usage rules.

Scarcity enforced at the component layer

Do

  • Use Hero once per marketing page, maximum.
  • Default to Primary for positive product actions.
  • Pair Destructive with Ghost in confirmation dialogs.
  • Use Ghost for "Cancel" next to Primary "Submit."
  • Use Neutral for low-stakes persistence (Save draft, Export).
  • Use icon-only buttons for repeated toolbar actions with clear iconography.
  • Always include aria-label on icon-only.

Don't

  • Don't use Hero in product surfaces (dashboards, tables, forms).
  • Don't use success, warning, or info as button colors. Those are alert and badge colors only.
  • Don't stack two Primary buttons side-by-side at equal emphasis. Pick one.
  • Don't use Destructive for reversible actions (Edit, Duplicate, Archive-that-can-be-unarchived).
  • Don't replace the text with a spinner in loading. Keep the label.
  • Don't add :focus ring without :focus-visible. Mouse clicks shouldn't flash the ring.
  • Don't override radius per-button. 7px is the system radius.
